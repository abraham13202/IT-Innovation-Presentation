🤖 Our Technology: Generative AI

What is Generative AI?

Generative AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that can create new content—text, code, insights, predictions—based on patterns learned from vast amounts of training data. Unlike traditional AI that classifies or predicts, GenAI generates novel outputs.

Core Technologies

  • Large Language Models (LLMs): GPT-4, Claude, PaLM
  • Transformer Architecture: Attention mechanisms for context understanding
  • Fine-tuning: Specialized training on financial data
  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation: Real-time data integration

Key Capabilities

  • Natural language understanding & generation
  • Pattern recognition in financial data
  • Contextual reasoning & predictions
  • Document analysis & synthesis
  • Multi-modal data processing

How FinSight AI Innovates with GenAI

4. Dominant Design Potential

Can GenAI Become the Dominant Design in Financial Advisory?

Dominant Design Theory (Utterback & Abernathy): A single architecture becomes the de facto standard after a period of experimentation.

✅ Evidence FOR Dominant Design

  • Industry Convergence: JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley all investing in similar GenAI architectures
  • Technical Standardization: Transformer models becoming universal (like PC architecture in computing)
  • Decreasing Experimentation: Moving from exploration to optimization phase
  • Regulatory Frameworks: Emerging standards (SEC AI guidelines) favor consistent approaches

⚠️ Challenges to Dominance

  • Rapid Evolution: AI technology still changing quickly
  • Competing Paradigms: Cloud-based LLMs vs on-premise models
  • Regulatory Uncertainty: Rules may favor diverse approaches
  • Customization Needs: Different institutions require different solutions

Our Position:

GenAI is emerging as dominant design for financial intelligence, but we're in the "era of ferment". FinSight aims to establish our platform architecture as the standard for SME financial advisory through:

  • Early market entry (first-mover advantage)
  • Open API standards (encouraging industry adoption)
  • Network effects (platform stickiness)
  • Regulatory compliance (becoming the "safe choice")
65% toward Dominant Design

Industry consensus emerging, but still evolving (2024 estimate)

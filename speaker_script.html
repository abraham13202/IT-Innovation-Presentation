FinSight AI - Investor Pitch Presentation

Detailed Speaker Script & Notes | Group 101, Tutorial 19

Presentation Overview

Total Duration: 10-12 minutes (adjust based on your tutorial requirements)

Format: Investment pitch for seed funding round

Tone: Professional, confident, data-driven

Key Message: We're democratizing financial intelligence by making AI-powered advisory accessible and affordable for SMEs

Key Message: Establish the problem (SMEs locked out of financial advisory) and preview our solution (AI-powered, affordable access).
Slide 2

The Problem

⏱ 90 seconds

Opening (Speaker: Abraham or Product lead)

What to say:

"Let's dive deeper into the problem we're solving. There are 32 million small and medium enterprises globally—businesses with 10 to 500 employees. These companies are the backbone of the economy, yet 85% of them lack access to sophisticated financial advisory services."

Current Barriers

"Why is this the case? There are four main barriers:"

  • Cost: "Traditional financial advisory firms charge upwards of $50,000 per year. For a small business with tight margins, this is simply not feasible."
  • Time: "Even if they try to do it themselves, manual financial analysis takes 20 to 40 hours every month—time that business owners don't have."
  • Quality: "The generic tools available—Excel spreadsheets, basic accounting software—provide no intelligent insights or personalized recommendations."
  • Complexity: "And navigating compliance and regulatory requirements requires expensive expertise that most SMEs can't afford."

Market Gap

"This has created a massive market gap. Big banks like JPMorgan and Goldman Sachs focus exclusively on large enterprises and high-net-worth individuals. Robo-advisors like Betterment only handle basic investment portfolio allocation. There is no comprehensive AI solution addressing the full spectrum of SME financial intelligence needs."

"This represents an $18 billion underserved market by 2028, and we're here to capture it."

Speaker Notes:
  • Use hand gestures to emphasize the four barriers
  • Pause after stating "$50,000 per year" to let it sink in
  • When mentioning competitors (JPMorgan, Goldman), acknowledge they're successful but emphasize they don't serve this market
  • End with confidence on the $18B market stat
Key Message: There's a huge, underserved market of SMEs that need financial advisory but can't afford or access current solutions. This is a real pain point with significant economic implications.

Slide 4

Technology: Generative AI

⏱ 90 seconds

Opening (Speaker: Technical lead - Vinayak)

What to say:

"Let me explain the technology that powers FinSight. At our core, we use Generative AI—specifically, Large Language Models similar to ChatGPT, but trained and optimized for financial applications."

What is GenAI?

"Generative AI differs from traditional AI in a crucial way: instead of just classifying data or making simple predictions, GenAI can understand context, reason through complex scenarios, and generate human-like insights. The technology includes:"

  • "Large Language Models that can process and understand financial documents, reports, and data"
  • "Natural language understanding so business owners can simply ask questions in plain English"
  • "Pattern recognition that identifies trends humans might miss"
  • "Contextual reasoning that considers multiple factors simultaneously"
  • "Content generation for reports, recommendations, and forecasts"

Our Innovation

"But here's what makes us different from just using ChatGPT or other generic AI:"

1. Finance-Specific Training: "We've fine-tuned our models on 10 years of financial statements from 50,000 companies, regulatory documents, market data, and industry benchmarks. This gives us 95% accuracy in financial document analysis, compared to 70% for generic LLMs."

2. Real-Time Data Integration: "We use a technique called Retrieval-Augmented Generation, or RAG. This connects our AI to live financial data—market feeds, economic indicators, your company's actual financial data. So you're not getting generic advice; you're getting current, context-aware recommendations."

3. Explainable AI: "Every recommendation our system makes comes with transparent reasoning. You can see exactly why the AI suggested a particular action, what data it considered, and its confidence level."

4. Compliance by Design: "We've built regulatory compliance directly into the AI architecture. The system won't make recommendations that violate financial regulations. This is critical for operating in a highly regulated industry."

"The results speak for themselves: 95% accuracy, 100 times faster than human analysts, and available 24/7."

Speaker Notes:
  • If presenting to technical audience, you can go deeper; if non-technical investors, keep it high-level
  • Use analogies: "Think of it like having a financial analyst with 10 years of experience available in your pocket, 24/7"
  • Emphasize the competitive moat: specialized training data and compliance architecture are difficult to replicate
  • The 95% vs 70% accuracy stat is powerful—highlight it
Key Message: Our technology is cutting-edge, purpose-built for finance, and provides real competitive advantages. We're not just wrapping a generic AI—we've built specialized, defensible technology.
Slide 5

Innovation Model - Platform & Open Innovation

⏱ 90 seconds

Opening (Speaker: Strategy lead - Abraham or Aditya)

What to say:

"Beyond our core technology, our business model itself is innovative. We're building FinSight as a platform, not just a product. Let me explain what this means and why it's strategically important."

Platform Innovation

"FinSight operates as a two-sided marketplace. On one side, we have SMEs and investors using our financial intelligence platform. On the other side, we have developers building specialized financial tools and applications."

"This creates powerful network effects. The more users we have, the better our AI models become through more data. Better models attract more users. More users attract more developers who want to reach this customer base. More developers create more valuable applications, which makes the platform stickier for users."

"This is exactly how successful platforms like Apple's App Store, Amazon Web Services, and Salesforce have built defensible moats. We're applying the same playbook to financial intelligence."

Open Innovation Strategy

"We're also embracing open innovation—the recognition that the best ideas come from everywhere, not just inside our company."

"On the inbound side, we're actively sourcing external knowledge:"

  • "University research partnerships with MIT and Stanford keep us at the cutting edge of AI research"
  • "We leverage open-source AI models as our foundation, then customize them"
  • "Customer co-creation: we involve our pilot users in product development"
  • "Data partnerships with Bloomberg and Refinitiv give us comprehensive market intelligence"

"On the outbound side, we selectively share innovation:"

  • "Our APIs are public, allowing developers to build on our platform"
  • "We're releasing anonymized financial benchmarks as open data—helping the entire SME ecosystem"
  • "We publish non-competitive research to establish thought leadership"
  • "We're building a developer community with hackathons and free API tiers"

"This open approach accelerates our innovation cycles, reduces R&D costs, and builds ecosystem loyalty. It's how we stay ahead despite being a startup competing with billion-dollar banks."

Speaker Notes:
  • This slide addresses the "platform innovation" and "open innovation" concepts required by your rubric
  • Draw parallel to successful platforms investors know: "Like how AWS provides infrastructure for developers, we provide financial intelligence infrastructure"
  • Emphasize network effects—this is a key driver of valuation in platform businesses
  • Balance: We're open to collaboration but protect core IP
Key Message: We're building a platform with network effects, not just a product. Our open innovation strategy accelerates growth while building a defensible moat.
Slide 6

Market Disruption - Dominant Design & Disruptive Innovation

⏱ 90 seconds

Opening (Speaker: Strategy lead - Abraham or Aditya)

What to say:

"Now let's talk about market dynamics. We're pursuing a classic low-end disruptive innovation strategy, as defined by Clayton Christensen in The Innovator's Dilemma."

Low-End Disruption Explained

"Look at the table on screen. Traditional financial advisory firms target large enterprises and charge $50,000 or more per year. Their business model is labor-intensive—expensive human advisors providing white-glove service."

"FinSight targets the market they ignore: SMEs that can't afford their services. We charge $199 per month—that's $2,400 per year, or less than 5% of traditional costs. Our business model is technology-enabled and scalable."

"This is textbook low-end disruption. We're entering at the bottom of the market with a 'good enough' solution for customers who are over-served by expensive alternatives or not served at all."

Why Incumbents Won't Respond

"Here's why this strategy works: Traditional advisory firms can't respond effectively, even though they see us coming."

"If they drop prices to compete with us, they'd cannibalize their high-margin services. Their CFO would say, 'Why would we give up $50,000 clients to chase $2,400 clients?' Their business model simply doesn't work at our price point."

"They view the SME market as unprofitable with their cost structure. They're rationally choosing to focus on their existing customers. But that gives us a long runway to establish dominance in this massive underserved market."

"And over time, as our AI improves through more data and feedback, we'll move upmarket. Eventually, we'll be competing for their high-end clients too—but by then, it'll be too late for them to catch up."

Dominant Design Emerging

"There's another dynamic at play: Generative AI, specifically transformer-based architectures, is emerging as the dominant design for financial intelligence applications."

"Just like the PC architecture became the standard in computing, or how iOS and Android became the dominant mobile platforms, we're seeing industry convergence around GenAI. JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Bank of America—they're all investing billions in similar GenAI architectures."

"We're positioning ourselves to ride this wave. By establishing our platform early, we can become the standard for SME financial advisory as the industry matures around this technology."

Speaker Notes:
  • This slide addresses "disruptive innovation" and "dominant design" concepts from your rubric
  • Use the table to clearly show the contrast between traditional and our approach
  • The "why incumbents won't respond" argument is crucial—address potential investor concern about competition
  • Reference Clayton Christensen by name—shows you understand innovation theory
  • The upmarket movement point is important: we start at the low end but won't stay there
Key Message: We're executing a proven disruption playbook. Incumbents are rationally ignoring us, giving us time to dominate the SME market before moving upmarket. GenAI is becoming the industry standard, and we're early.

Business Model

⏱ 75 seconds

Opening (Speaker: CFO - Aditya)

What to say:

"Let me walk you through our business model and unit economics. We've designed a model that's profitable at scale with strong margins."

Value Proposition & Customer Segments

"Our value proposition is simple: 90% cost reduction versus traditional advisory, with institutional-grade intelligence, 24/7 accessibility, and built-in compliance."

"We target four customer segments:"

  • "Primary: SMEs with 10 to 500 employees—this is our core market"
  • "Independent financial advisors who want to augment their services with AI"
  • "Retail investors managing $50K to $1M portfolios"
  • "Fintech platforms that want to embed our intelligence in their products—this is B2B"

Revenue Streams

"We have four revenue streams:"

  1. "SaaS subscriptions are our primary revenue source at 80%. Pricing starts at $199 per month for small businesses, scaling up to $999 per month for larger enterprises."
  2. "API usage fees at 10% of revenue. Developers pay per query to access our AI models."
  3. "Platform commission at 5%. When third-party developers sell apps on our marketplace, we take a 30% commission, similar to the App Store model."
  4. "Premium services at 5%. Custom model training, white-glove onboarding, consulting services."

Unit Economics

"Our unit economics are strong and improving. We start at $199 per month, which is $2,400 in annual revenue per customer. By Year 3, we project:"

  • "75% gross margins—typical for SaaS businesses"
  • "Customer Lifetime Value of $21,600"
  • "Customer Acquisition Cost of just $600"
  • "That's a 36-to-1 LTV to CAC ratio—anything above 3-to-1 is considered excellent"

"The economics work because our marginal costs decrease with scale. AI inference costs drop as technology improves, while our models get better with more data. This creates a powerful flywheel."

Speaker Notes:
  • This addresses the "Business Model Canvas" requirement from your rubric
  • The LTV:CAC ratio is a key metric investors care about—emphasize 36:1 is exceptional
  • Gross margins of 75% show this is a high-quality SaaS business
  • Multiple revenue streams reduce risk; subscription is stable and predictable
  • If asked about the full Business Model Canvas, you can expand on key partners (cloud providers, data vendors), key activities (AI training, platform development), etc.
Key Message: We have a diversified, high-margin business model with excellent unit economics. This is a capital-efficient business that becomes more profitable at scale.

Final Presentation Tips

Practice & Timing

  • Practice the full presentation at least 5 times before the actual pitch
  • Time yourselves—make sure you hit 10-12 minutes total
  • Practice transitions between speakers smoothly
  • Have backup plans if technology fails (can you present without slides?)

Delivery

  • Make eye contact with the audience, not just the slides
  • Speak clearly and at a measured pace—don't rush
  • Use hand gestures naturally to emphasize points
  • Show passion and enthusiasm, but maintain professionalism
  • If you make a mistake, don't apologize profusely—just move on

Engagement

  • Start with a hook: "Imagine being a small business owner..." (done in slide 1)
  • Use rhetorical questions: "Why is this the case?" (done throughout)
  • Tell micro-stories: examples of hypothetical customers helped by your product
  • Use pauses for emphasis: after key statistics, let them sink in
  • Vary your tone and energy—don't be monotone

Team Coordination

  • Decide who presents each slide ahead of time
  • Practice handoffs: "Now I'll hand it over to [Name] who will discuss our technology"
  • Support each other: nod along when teammates are speaking
  • For Q&A, decide who's best positioned to answer different types of questions

Innovation Concepts Coverage

Make sure you explicitly mention these required concepts:

  • ✓ Platform Innovation: Covered in Slide 5 (two-sided marketplace, network effects)
  • ✓ Open Innovation: Covered in Slide 5 (inbound/outbound strategy)
  • ✓ Crowdsourcing/Open Data: Mentioned in Slide 5 (community benchmarks, open datasets)
  • ✓ Web API: Covered throughout (API marketplace, developer ecosystem)
  • ✓ Dominant Design: Covered in Slide 6 (GenAI architecture becoming standard)
  • ✓ Disruptive Innovation: Covered in Slide 6 (low-end disruption analysis)
  • ✓ Value Chain: Implicit in Slides 3 & 7 (where we fit in financial advisory chain)
  • ✓ Business Model Canvas: Covered in Slide 7 (value prop, customers, revenue, resources)
